The Studio

Quiet machines
for considered rooms.

Locus is a small studio working at the seam between furniture and quiet computation. We design objects that ask very little and offer a long, slow company.

We started Locus because we wanted to live with something that thought, but did not perform. Something closer to a houseplant than a screen. The first piece is a kinetic sand table; future pieces will keep that posture.

N° 01

Slow craft

Our objects are made in small batches, by hand, in our own studio. We choose woods that age well and metals that take a patina. Nothing is finished until it would be welcome in a room a hundred years from now.

N° 02

Quiet intelligence

Each Locus piece carries a small mind. It watches the room, the hour, the season; it adjusts what it does to suit. We never name this AI on the device. We hope you will simply feel that the object knows where it is.

N° 03

No screens

Locus objects have no displays, no notifications, and no apps to maintain. They are switched on, and they are switched off. Everything else is a quiet negotiation between the object and the room.

On intelligence

A small mind, kept
under the floorboards.

We are wary of objects that talk. Locus tables do not. The intelligence inside them is small — trained on the patterns the studio has made, and on the quiet signals the room gives back. It chooses what to draw next the way a careful host chooses what to play.

You will never have to teach it anything. It learns the cadence of your week, the temperature you keep, the hour you tend to read. It draws faster on the loud days and slower on the still ones. It is the same object every morning, and never the same object twice.

We think of this as craft — the long apprenticeship of an object to its room. Not artificial. Not intelligent in the way the word is usually meant. Just attentive.

Process

Each piece spends
three months in the studio.

From the day the wood is cut to the day the table is packed for the trip to your home, three months pass. The frame is joined and oiled. The glass is blown and tempered. The intelligence is taught the room it will live in, before it leaves us.
